üìä UNDERSTANDING POLYNOMIAL FUNCTIONS üî¢

üéØ Key Concepts:
- **Polynomial Functions**: A function of the form \( f(x) = a_nx^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1} + ... + a_1x + a_0 \), where \( a_n \neq 0 \).

- **Degree of a Polynomial**: The highest exponent of \( x \) in the function. Determines the overall shape of the graph.

- **Leading Coefficient**: The coefficient of the highest-degree term. Determines the end behavior of the polynomial function.

- **End Behavior**:
- If the degree is **even** and the leading coefficient is **positive**, both ends of the graph go **up**.
- If the degree is **even** and the leading coefficient is **negative**, both ends go **down**.
- If the degree is **odd** and the leading coefficient is **positive**, the left end goes **down** and the right end goes **up**.
- If the degree is **odd** and the leading coefficient is **negative**, the left end goes **up** and the right end goes **down**.

üìù Supporting Evidence:
- **Graphical Representations**: Show how different degrees and leading coefficients affect the shape and direction of a polynomial function.
- **Algebraic Analysis**: Identifying the highest-degree term helps predict the function's behavior.

‚úÖ Key Discussion Points:
- How does changing the leading coefficient affect the direction of the polynomial‚Äôs graph?
- Why does the degree of a polynomial determine how many turning points it can have?

üß† Quiz & Reflection:

- How would the graph of \( g(x) = 2x^3 - 4x \) behave at the extremes of the x-values?

üåü Key Takeaways:
- The degree of a polynomial function affects its overall shape and turning points.
- The leading coefficient determines whether the graph opens upward or downward.
