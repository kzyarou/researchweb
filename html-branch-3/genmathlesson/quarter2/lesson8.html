üéØ Key Concepts:
- **Simple Interest (SI)**: Interest calculated on the principal only.
- **Compound Interest (CI)**: Interest calculated on both the principal and previously accumulated interest.

- **Formula for Simple Interest**:
\[ SI = P \times r \times t \] where:
- \( SI \) = Simple interest
- \( P \) = Principal amount
- \( r \) = Annual interest rate (decimal form)
- \( t \) = Time in years

- **Formula for Compound Interest**:
\[ A = P(1 + r/n)^{nt} \] where:
- \( A \) = Final amount (Principal + Interest)
- \( P \) = Principal amount
- \( r \) = Annual interest rate (decimal form)
- \( n \) = Number of times interest is compounded per year
- \( t \) = Time in years

- **Differences Between SI & CI**:
- Simple interest grows linearly, while compound interest grows exponentially.
- Compound interest generates more earnings over time, especially with frequent compounding.

üìù Supporting Evidence:
- **Savings Accounts**: Compound interest allows money to grow faster over time.
- **Loans & Mortgages**: Understanding interest types helps in making better financial decisions.

üìà CALCULATING FUTURE VALUE OF INVESTMENTS üí∞

üéØ Key Concepts:
- **Future Value (FV)**: The value of an investment after interest has been applied over time.
- **Simple Interest Formula**:
\[ FV = P + (P \times r \times t) \] where:
- \( P \) = Principal amount
- \( r \) = Annual interest rate (decimal form)
- \( t \) = Time in years

- **Compound Interest Formula**:
\[ FV = P(1 + r/n)^{nt} \] where:
- \( n \) = Number of times interest is compounded per year

- **Key Differences**:
- Simple interest grows at a constant rate.
- Compound interest grows exponentially, leading to higher returns over time.

üìù Supporting Evidence:
- Real-life applications include bank savings, investment accounts, and retirement funds.

‚úÖ Key Discussion Points:
- Why does compounding increase future value more than simple interest?
- How does increasing the compounding frequency affect the final investment value?

üß† Quiz & Reflection:
- If you invest $5,000 at 6% annual interest, compounded monthly for 5 years, what will be the future value?
- Compare the difference in returns between an investment using simple interest and one using compound interest over 10 years.

üåü Key Takeaways:
- Future value depends on the type of interest applied (simple vs. compound).
- Compound interest provides greater growth potential for long-term investments.
