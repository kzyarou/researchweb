üìö UNDERSTANDING VARIATION: DIRECT, INVERSE, JOINT, AND COMBINED üìä

üéØ Key Concepts:
- **Direct Variation**: When one quantity increases, the other also increases proportionally. Expressed as y = kx, where k is the constant of proportionality.
- **Inverse Variation**: When one quantity increases, the other decreases proportionally. Expressed as y = k/x.
- **Joint Variation**: A variable depends on two or more other variables, expressed as y = kxz (e.g., area of a triangle A = 1/2 bh).
- **Combined Variation**: A mix of direct and inverse variations within the same equation.

üìù Real-Life Applications:
- **Direct Variation**: Salary based on hours worked, speed and distance.
- **Inverse Variation**: The time needed to complete a job when more workers are added.
- **Joint Variation**: The volume of a gas varies jointly with temperature and pressure.
- **Combined Variation**: The brightness of a lightbulb depending on both voltage and resistance.

‚úÖ Key Discussion Points:
- How can you determine the type of variation from a given data set or equation?
- What are some real-world examples where multiple variations occur simultaneously?

üß† Quiz & Reflection:
- If y varies directly as x and y = 24 when x = 6, find the equation and solve for y when x = 10.
- A machine completes a task in 10 hours with 4 workers. If the number of workers doubles, how long will the task take?

üåü Key Takeaways:
- Identifying the type of variation helps in solving problems efficiently.
- Variation principles apply to physics, economics, and engineering.
